Part I · The Talmudic foundation

The Three Oaths.

Before sending the Jewish people into exile, G-d made them swear three oaths governing their conduct through diaspora. The first two forbid what Zionism does.

Kesubos 111a · 8 min · Rashi · Rambam · Maharal
+

The Talmud (Kesubos 111a) records that Hashem imposed three oaths on the Jewish people in exile. These oaths are not optional pieties — they are binding halachic obligations cited by every major authority from the Rambam forward.

"That Jews shall not ascend to the Land en masse as a political movement, and that Jews shall not rebel against the nations of the world."Kesubos 111a · Oaths 1 & 2

The Zionist state was established in direct contradiction of the first two oaths. This is not a fringe interpretation — it is the plain reading cited by the Rambam, the Ramban, the Maharal, and every major posek through the 20th century.

ii
Part II · The magnum opus

Vayoel Moshe.

The Satmar Rebbe's 1961 three-treatise work — the canonical halachic case against Zionism. Still taught, still distributed, still unanswered.

Published 1959–1961 · 14 min · Three treatises
+

Vayoel Moshe is the Satmar Rebbe's systematic halachic response to the Zionist claim that establishing the State of Israel was religiously permitted — even religiously praiseworthy.

Across three major essays — on the Three Oaths, on the prohibition of settling the Land by force, and on the Hebrew language — the Rebbe lays out the sources from Talmud, Rishonim, and Acharonim with total textual rigor.

"The Zionists, with all their deeds, have led the Jewish people into the greatest catastrophe since the destruction of the Second Temple."Satmar Rebbe · Vayoel Moshe

The work has never been seriously refuted on its own terms.

Part III · The chorus of voices

Voices of the Gedolim.

Zionism was never opposed by one man. It was opposed by the entire mainstream of European Torah leadership — across every tradition.

10 authorities cited · 11 min · Published works
+

From the first Zionist Congress in 1897 through the present day, every major Torah authority who addressed the question in writing condemned it. Not "most." Not "many." All.

This includes figures as different as the Chofetz Chaim, Reb Chaim Brisker, the Chazon Ish, Rabbi Samson Raphael Hirsch, the Munkacser Rebbe, the earlier Lubavitcher Rebbes, and the Ben Ish Chai. The agreement across Chasidic, Litvish, German-Orthodox, and Sephardi traditions is itself halachically decisive.

Part V · The questions journalists keep asking

Frequently asked.

"Do all Jews support Israel?" "Isn't anti-Zionism antisemitism?" Direct answers to what the press asks most.

8 common questions · 5 min · Answers with sources
+

"Do all Jews support Israel?" — No. Hundreds of thousands of Torah-observant Jews, and the entire Torah tradition before 1948, reject political Zionism.

"Isn't anti-Zionism antisemitism?" — The conflation is the Zionist movement's own rhetorical invention. Opposing a modern political state is not opposing the Jewish people — the Torah itself forbids what Zionism does.

"Aren't you just Neturei Karta?" — Neturei Karta is one expression of the broader Natruna tradition. Torah Jews is a public-facing project of that wider community, founded in 2003 at the request of senior Satmar rabbis.
